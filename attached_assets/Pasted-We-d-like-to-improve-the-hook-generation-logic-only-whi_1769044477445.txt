We’d like to improve the hook-generation logic only, while leaving the rest of the LinkedIn Content Workflow Engine unchanged.

This is not a redesign — it’s a refinement.

GOAL

Increase “See more” clicks and early engagement by making hooks:

More specific

More opinionated

More tension-driven
while staying calm, credible, and non-hype.

Hooks should feel like earned authority, not marketing copy.

CURRENT ISSUE

Hooks are directionally correct but sometimes:

Too neutral

Too safe

Not distinct enough between posts

Lacking specificity (numbers, contrasts, rules)

We want hooks that feel bookmarkable and disagreeable-in-a-good-way.

UPDATED HOOK RULES (PLEASE IMPLEMENT)
1. First Two Lines Are Mandatory Hooks

Each post must generate:

Line 1 (Hook)

Line 2 (Rehook)

Optimized for LinkedIn’s “See more” cutoff.

2. Hook Length & Structure

Aim for ≤ 8 words per line

Short, declarative sentences

No questions in Line 1

No emojis

No hype language

3. Hook Types to Bias Toward

For each post, prefer one of the following hook styles:

A. Strong Contrarian Statement

“AI agents won’t replace departments.”

“Early AI adoption isn’t about intelligence.”

B. Operator Pain Mirror

“Busy all day. Nothing moved.”

“Most teams aren’t inefficient — they’re overwhelmed.”

C. Rule or Principle

“Coordination pain is the real trigger.”

“AI only sticks when chaos disappears.”

D. Reframe / Contrast

“Not smarter AI. Quieter execution.”

“Relief beats innovation.”

4. Second Line (Rehook) Requirements

Line 2 should:

Add clarity or tension

Narrow the audience

Extend curiosity without mystery

Examples:

“That’s why operators adopt AI first.”

“This is where most AI strategies break.”

5. Avoid These Patterns

Do NOT generate hooks that:

Sound generic or instructional (“Here’s why…”, “Let’s talk about…”)

Use vague abstractions (“the future”, “game-changing”)

Ask rhetorical questions in the first line

Repeat the same hook pattern across all four posts

POST-TYPE-SPECIFIC HOOK BIAS

Apply slight variation by post type:

Educational Authority
→ Strong stance or reframing

Founder Story
→ Emotional mirror or lived quote

Trend Translation
→ Disagreement with prevailing narrative

System Principle
→ Clear rule, constraint, or belief

IMPORTANT CONSTRAINTS

Do NOT change:

Post body generation

Context logic

Feedback system

Google Sheets export

Only adjust hook and rehook generation logic

Hooks must still respect:

Calm, authoritative tone

Operator-first audience

“AI Employees / system” positioning

SUCCESS CRITERIA

A good hook should make a qualified reader think:

“That’s exactly what I’m dealing with — keep going.”

Not:

“That sounds smart.”
